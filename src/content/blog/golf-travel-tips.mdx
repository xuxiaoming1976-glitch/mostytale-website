Flying with golf clubs doesn't have to be stressful. With the right preparation and a quality travel bag, your clubs can arrive safely at any destination. Here's our complete guide.

## Before You Pack

Take these precautions before your clubs go into the travel bag:

- **Remove adjustable heads** from drivers and fairway woods
- **Wrap iron heads** with towels or headcovers for extra padding
- **Use a stiff arm** travel rod to absorb vertical impacts
- **Take photos** of your clubs for documentation

## The Packing Process

1. Place your golf bag inside the travel case
2. Secure it firmly with the internal compression straps
3. Fill empty spaces with shoes, towels, or clothing for extra padding
4. Zip up completely and double-check all buckles

## At the Airport

- Check in at least 90 minutes before departure
- Consider a TSA-approved lock (non-locking preferred for inspection)
- Most major airlines treat golf bags as standard checked luggage
- Weight limits vary — check your airline's policy

## Arrival Check

Before leaving baggage claim, unzip your travel bag and inspect your clubs immediately. If you find damage, document it with photos and file a claim at the airline's baggage office right away.

## Conclusion

A little preparation goes a long way. With a quality travel bag and smart packing, your clubs are as safe in the air as they are in your trunk.
