Golf club grooves are precision-engineered channels designed to channel moisture, grass, and debris away from the club face at impact. When those grooves get clogged with dirt and grass, your performance suffers — and the data proves it.

## The Science of Spin

Research from Golf Laboratories shows that dirty grooves can reduce spin by 30-50% on wedge shots. Here's what that means in practical terms:

- **Less stopping power** on firm greens
- **Reduced distance control** due to higher, less predictable launch
- **Poorer contact consistency** across the face

## By the Numbers

A study comparing clean vs. dirty wedges found:

| Metric | Clean Grooves | Dirty Grooves | Difference |
|--------|--------------|---------------|------------|
| Spin (RPM) | 8,500 | 5,200 | -39% |
| Launch Angle | 28° | 32° | +14% |
| Shot Dispersion | ±3.2 yds | ±5.4 yds | +69% |

## Tour Pro Habits

Watch any PGA Tour event and you'll notice: pros clean their clubs constantly. Some clean after every single shot. It's not obsessive — it's strategic. They know that consistency comes from clean grooves.

## How to Build a Cleaning Habit

1. **Pre-round**: Fill your MOSTY cleaner bag with water
2. **Every shot**: 10 seconds to clean the club you just used
3. **Between holes**: Quick ball clean for better putting
4. **Post-round**: Wipe down all clubs before storing

## The Bottom Line

You wouldn't play with dirty golf balls. You shouldn't play with dirty clubs either. A clean club is a consistent club — and consistency is what lowers scores.
